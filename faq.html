Frequently Asked Questions

Here are the answers to most of the questions that drivers frequently have. If you have any additional questions, contact us!

How long are Gurney drivers out?

40% of our drivers are home daily. The rest of our drivers are home every 3-10 days depending on the route.

How much does Gurney Trucking pay?

Our drivers make on average $57,000 - $100,000 or more.

What areas do you run?

Our hauls span from the Mountain West into California, Texas, and the Midwest.

What types of positions do you offer?

We have both slip seat as well as dedicated lane positions open, both for Solo and Team Dedicated Routes!

What type of equipment do you have?

We run Kenworths, Peterbilts, and Frightliners. Our trucks are on average 1.5 years old, with none of them being older than 3 years old.

What type of freight do you haul?

We haul livestock and dry freight. We offer training to help drivers know how to haul livestock.

As an experienced driver, how much experience do I need?

We prefer drivers with at least two years of experience. If you don't have two years yet, we have great training options for drivers with their CDL. Typically, we prefer driversr with 6 months of experience for the training program

Do you require HAZMAT?

Nope, we don't require a HAZMAT endorsement.

Do you have a friendly pet program?

You betcha! We understand the benefit of bringing your companion along, we just require a cleaning deposit to make sure the truck is ready for the driver.

Do you have a rider program?

You betcha! There are a few forms to fill out ahead of time before a rider can get on the truck.

Where are your terminals located?

We have Utah terminals in St. George and Aurora. We also have a Nevada terminal in Las Vegas.

How long is orientation for new drivers?

We understand orientation can be a drag when you are ready to get working, so we keep orientation to half a day.

What bonuses or incentives does Gurney Trucking offer their drivers?

We offer quarterly fuel bonuses and safety bonuses to every driver. We also offer a number of paid vacation days.

What work benefits does Gurney Trucking offer their drivers?

We off health, dental, and vision insurance. We also cover 100% of our drivers short-term disability insurnace.

Will Gurney Trucking help me get my CDL?

No, we require our drivers to already have a CDL. However, we do offer training for drivers with little experience.
